About this project

The Presidential Coverage Tracker is a research project from Yale political scientists that measures the tone of presidential coverage across major US broadcast and cable news network and digital news outlets.

We collect closed-caption transcripts from the Internet Archive's TV News Archive, classify each Trump-mention segment using a fine-tuned natural language inference model, and publish weekly updates to this dashboard. News headlines are collected from Media Cloud, a global news database.

Traditional methods of measuring media tone, including dictionary-based word counts and transformer-based sentiment classifiers, assign an overall positive, negative, or neutral score to a document rather than to the candidates it discusses. This document-level approach cannot identify to whom sentiment is directed. A headline such as 'Biden struggles to contain inflation' conveys a markedly different signal about the president than 'Inflation eases under Biden,' yet both might be treated as negative in a dictionary-based model that flags 'inflation' as a negative term. We adapt methods from natural language inference (NLI) to measure candidate-specific media coverage. Rather than inferring tone from surface-level word usage, our approach uses stance detection to evaluate whether a headline or transcript segment implies that a given candidate is performing well or poorly.

Data & Methodology

Each transcript is split into 3-sentence windows. Windows mentioning "Trump" are classified using the Political DEBATE model developed by Michael Burnham. We further train the Political DEBATE model on a task that evaluates whether the text conveys a positive, negative, or neutral performance cue about Trump.

A segment is classified as positive if the model scores a "yes" on the hypothesis "the author of this text believes that Trump is performing/performed/will perform well" and negative if the model scores a "yes" on the hypothesis "the author of this text believes that Trump is performing/performed/will perform poorly."

The weekly net score is computed as: % positive minus % negative chunks that week.

The same method applies for digital news coverage using the headline as the unit of analysis.

For more information about the methodology, including the assessment of the reliability and validity of our measure, please view our working paper.

Networks covered

CBS, CNN, Fox News, ABC, NBC, and MSNBC/MSNow. Data runs from January 2025 to the present and is updated weekly.

We include national broadcast and cable news programs only — no local affiliates or regional newscasts. For each network we selected flagship news, morning, evening, and primetime opinion programs that reach a national audience.

Digital news outlets covered

Reuters, Fox News, CBS News, Bloomberg, CNN, ABC News, USA Today, New York Times, NBC News, Los Angeles Times, and NPR. Headlines are collected via the Media Cloud API.
