About this project

The Presidential Coverage Tracker is a research project from Yale political scientists that measures the tone of Trump coverage across major US broadcast and cable news networks.

We collect closed-caption transcripts from the Internet Archive's TV News Archive, classify each Trump-mention segment using a fine-tuned natural language inference model, and publish weekly updates to this dashboard.

Data & Methodology

Each transcript is split into 3-sentence windows. Windows mentioning "Trump" are classified using the Political DEBATE model developed by Michael Burnham. A segment is classified as positive (+1) if the model scores the "performing well" hypothesis above 0.5, negative (-1) for "performing poorly," or neutral (0) otherwise.

The weekly net score is computed as: % positive minus % negative chunks that week.

Networks covered

CBS, CNN, Fox News, ABC, NBC, and MSNBC/MSNow. Data runs from January 2025 to the present and is updated weekly.
